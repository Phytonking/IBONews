This Project

This project is a r/IBO Newspaper. This way, members can share their writings and their talents, such as drawing, and even music. Not only these, but this newspaper can allow members to share their stories, tips, and help one another, which is essentially the purpose of this server. This can serve as a platform for students to explore issues that are important to them and their peers, such as social justice, mental health, academic stress, and the IBO, of course.

This project will not only help bring the community together, but it will also help students develop their writing, editing, and digital media skills, which can be beneficial for the diploma programme, and even possibly their future academic and professional careers. Partaking in this project can help members to promote critical thinking, awareness, and advocacy, for things they're interested about.

We've passed the 50k member threshold, which means the server is now bigger than a small town, as defined by the US Census Bureau. Every small town has its own publication, and this would be ours. This can take our community a step forward.

Furthermore, this project can be used as a CAS project inside the server, as explained later (see here).

Where will this project be taking place?

The pieces will be published here: https://ibo.pythonanywhere.com - the website is made with sample posts and texts, just for the staff to get the feel of this project when it’s running. The aesthetic aspect of the website can be improved as this project continues. If met with enough support, there can be people appointed as website managers, just to improve the looks of the website. This Website is best viewed on PC/Laptop.

If the mods have any issues with any of the sections on the website, we're more than happy to take the suggestions on board.

Our Discord: https://discord.gg/ibo

The rationale for a CAS project inside the server:

The assistance provided through the CAS channel is limited. Despite other channels offering help to the IB students, the CAS channel is commonly filled with responses such as "ask your coordinator," which is made light of in the channel's description, as well.

Additionally, people often seek project ideas in the channel, which contravenes rule 5 concerning academic dishonesty, and some individuals use the channel to promote their own CAS projects, which violates rule 10. By incorporating a CAS project section within the server, we can provide assistance to all three groups mentioned and also direct people to this section when they seek project ideas.
